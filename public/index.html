About Me

I started to work as a developer at the age of 14, I've been involved in several projects since 1999.

As a young developer, I lived the dot-com bubble, working in a very innovative Chilean project founded by Sillicon Valley's Cyclop Technologies Inc, in an artifitial intelligence, or search engine, related project.

Always interested in technology, I started to work at VTR, developing applications for the first broadband provider in Chile from 2001-2004. After that, in 2004 I started to work in Tarreo, a Chilean LanParty version, being part of the organisation of events up to 1000 gamers.

In 2006 I founded my own development services company called AmazinWorks, developing several web related projects for important Chilean companies until 2010.

In 2007 I was part of a educational game development team called "Coleoptero" founded by Fernando Flores, where I was the project leader in a Flash Multiplayer game called "Invención 2065." It was featured on BBC and was one of the best innovation project in Chile that year.

At the end of 2008, I also worked as developer for Tigabytes, a Google Enterprise Partner in Chile.

In 2010 I decided to create an startup with an innovation managment software, but we couldn't find customers or raise money in time.

At the end of 2010 I worked as backend developer at Piccsy.com, a social network for image bookmarking and a Start-Up Chile first-round team.

In 2011 I worked as a consultant, leading a developing team working as CTO in a local company called BrandWeb.

Currently in 2012, I'm working as a CTO at StudioSnaps, and offering software architecture and development consultancy for several startups and local companies.
